# Hiraeth

> *hiraeth* (Welsh) — a longing for home, for something that was or never was. A grief for the familiar, the warm, the ordinary made precious by time.

Hiraeth is a procedurally generated living village simulator. A small world made of people — going to work, falling in love, growing old, building things, breaking things. You watch from above, or walk among them. The world breathes without you.

## What It Is

A 3D isometric town simulation built entirely in code — no editor, no prefabs, just geometry, systems, and emergence. Every character has a name, a role, a home, a schedule. The baker opens at dawn. The old man feeds the ducks. The teenagers hang around outside the chip shop on Friday evenings.

The town is **procedurally generated** — terrain shaped by noise, buildings placed by constraint, characters seeded with personality and history. You can also load the shape of a **real place** from map data and watch it come alive.

## Core Experience

- **Isometric view** with smooth zoom — pull back to see the whole valley, lean in to read the expression on someone's face
- **Follow any character** — their day unfolds: morning routines, work, lunch, relationships, arguments, quiet moments
- **Day/night cycle** — the town changes character as the sun moves; pubs fill, lights come on in windows, the last bus home
- **Seasons and weather** — rain turns the streets dark, snow quiets everything, summer brings people outside
- **Living history** — characters are born, grow up, get jobs, fall out with neighbours, have kids, get old, and die. The town remembers

## The Feeling

Cozy but not saccharine. The warmth comes from the specificity of ordinary life. You'll find yourself rooting for people you never controlled. Stories emerge you couldn't have written. That's the point.
